SNAP Recipients Could Lose Benefits by November: Why is This a Top Concern for New Yorkers?

The ongoing federal government shutdown could affect SNAP recipients' aid if it continues by Nov. 1, and the new SNAP provisions could impact eligibility for 300,000 households in New York.

By: Samantha Vaquero

Oct. 27, 2025

President Donald Trump signed the One Big Beautiful Bill Act into law in July, which reduces federal funding for the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) by $186 billion through 2034. The law also includes new provisions, like expanded work requirements, and it requires states to contribute to the cost of benefits.

In 2024, approximately 41.7 million people per month received SNAP benefits, and as of now, about 3 million New York residents receive SNAP benefits. Due to the new provisions, 300,000 households could lose some or all of their SNAP benefits due to the modified work requirements for able-bodied adults without dependents (ABAWD), according to a statement by Governor Kathy Hochul.

Losing SNAP benefits for New York City residents would add another cost to their monthly expenses, especially at a time when 61% of New Yorkers say it is becoming increasingly difficult to meet basic needs such as housing, food, healthcare, and education, according to a recent survey.
